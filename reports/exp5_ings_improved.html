Experiment 5: Improved INGS Variants

Date: 2026-05-19  |  Runtime: 186.6 min

1. Motivation

Experiment 4 showed INGS matches TPE/BOHB on smooth XOMO landscapes but lags on small datasets (nasa93dem, POM3) due to a noisy 20%-split validation proxy. This experiment tests four targeted improvements and their combination.

5. Discussion

5.1 CV proxy (INGS-CV)

The k=2 fold CV proxy uses all training data for proxy estimation, trading fewer exploration steps (15 vs 30 configs) for a substantially more stable signal. This should most benefit small datasets (nasa93dem, POM3) where the 20% fixed split was too noisy. The cost is fewer steps for the surrogate to update and refine.

5.2 UCB exploration bonus (INGS-UCB)

The UCB term adds κ·min_dist(h, observed)/√d to the score. With κ=0.1, the bonus is proportional to the normalized distance from the nearest evaluated config. This prevents the search from over-exploiting a small promising region when the surrogate may not extrapolate well.

5.3 Multi-fold final selection (INGS-Multi)

After the search, the top-3 candidates by val R² are re-evaluated on a fresh (different random seed) validation split. The best by this second estimate is chosen. This two-stage selection reduces the influence of a lucky/unlucky single split on the final pick, at the cost of 3 additional model trains.

5.4 LHS initialization (INGS-LHS)

Latin Hypercube Sampling provides a space-filling design over the 5D HPO space. Uniform random initialization can cluster by chance; LHS guarantees at most one sample per interval on each dimension. A better-spread initial set gives the RBF interpolant more reliable gradient estimates from the start.

5.5 Combined (INGS-Full)

INGS-Full applies all improvements simultaneously. If the improvements are largely orthogonal (CV addresses proxy noise; LHS addresses initialization quality; UCB addresses exploration-exploitation; Multi-fold addresses selection noise), their combination should be synergistic.
