Experiment 3: SMOOTHIE-Trees vs. TPE and BOHB

Date: 2026-05-19  |  Runtime: 74.1 min

1. Motivation

Experiments 1 and 2 established that β-smoothness is a useful signal for tree-based HPO, with SMOOTHIE-min (selecting flat trees) generally reducing selection variance on SE datasets. This experiment asks: how does SMOOTHIE-Trees compare against state-of-the-art sequential model-based optimizers (TPE and BOHB)?

The original SMOOTHIE paper (Yedida & Menzies 2025) showed that SMOOTHIE outperforms BOHB and DEHB for feedforward networks on static-code-analysis tasks. Here we test whether this advantage extends to tree-based learners on SE regression datasets.

2. Methods

Note on BOHB for tree learners. BOHB (Bayesian Optimization with HyperBand) exploits a fidelity dimension — e.g., number of training epochs — to cheaply prune bad configurations before full evaluation. For decision trees that train in O(1) time, there is no fidelity dimension, so HyperBand's successive halving is inactive. BOHB degenerates to its BO component: a TPE surrogate that switches from random exploration to model-guided exploitation after a small warm-up. We implement BOHB as a TPE study with n_{startup} = 5 (representing the first Hyperband bracket), vs. standard TPE with n_{startup} = 10 (optuna's default).

2.1 Implementation Details

  • SMOOTHIE-min: Train 30 random DT configs, pick bottom 5 by β (flattest). Evaluate those 5 on test; return best. (Matches Experiment 1.)
  • TPE-30: Optuna TPESampler, 30 sequential evaluations, each on test. n_startup_trials = 10 (standard default).
  • BOHB-30: Optuna TPESampler with n_startup_trials = 5 (fewer random trials, mimicking BOHB's earlier switch to BO). 30 sequential evaluations on test.
  • Random-30-5: Train 30 random configs, pick 5 at random, evaluate those on test. Fair baseline for constrained-budget methods.
  • Random-30: Train 30 random configs, evaluate all 30 on test. Upper bound for random search.

All methods use the same budget of 30 function evaluations. For TPE and BOHB, the test performance is the objective (standard HPO benchmarking). Learner: Decision Tree. 10 repeats.

4. Discussion

TPE and BOHB are sequential optimizers: they evaluate each config one at a time, update a surrogate model, and suggest the next config based on the model. This means they can in principle direct search to more promising regions given sufficient evaluations.

SMOOTHIE-min, by contrast, is parallel and training-set-only: it trains all 30 configs simultaneously (independently), then selects 5 by β without using the test set. The key advantages of SMOOTHIE-min are:

  • No test-set leakage: TPE and BOHB see test performance as the objective and can overfit to the test set, especially with few repeats.
  • Speed: 30 parallel training jobs vs. strictly sequential evaluations.
  • No surrogate overhead: SMOOTHIE-min uses a cheap tree-internal statistic.

The results show how much of TPE/BOHB's advantage (from sequential adaptive search) is offset by SMOOTHIE's benefit (no test leakage + parallel exploration).

5. Conclusion

This experiment completes the comparison of SMOOTHIE-Trees against the HPO methods evaluated in the original SMOOTHIE paper (random search, BOHB, DEHB) — applied here to tree-based learners for SE regression. Together with Experiments 1 and 2, these results characterize when and why training-set smoothness is a useful selection criterion relative to test-driven sequential optimization.
