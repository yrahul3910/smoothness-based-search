Experiment 3: SMOOTHIE-Trees vs. TPE and BOHB

Date: 2026-05-19  |  Runtime: 74.0 min

1. Motivation

Experiments 1 and 2 established that β-smoothness is a useful signal for tree-based HPO, with SMOOTHIE-min (selecting flat trees) generally reducing selection variance on SE datasets. This experiment asks: how does SMOOTHIE-Trees compare against state-of-the-art sequential model-based optimizers (TPE and BOHB)?

The original SMOOTHIE paper (Yedida & Menzies 2025) showed that SMOOTHIE outperforms BOHB and DEHB for feedforward networks on static-code-analysis tasks. Here we test whether this advantage extends to tree-based learners on SE regression datasets.

2. Methods

Note on BOHB for tree learners. BOHB (Bayesian Optimization with HyperBand) exploits a fidelity dimension — e.g., number of training epochs — to cheaply prune bad configurations before full evaluation. For decision trees that train in O(1) time, there is no fidelity dimension, so HyperBand's successive halving is inactive. BOHB degenerates to its BO component: a TPE surrogate that switches from random exploration to model-guided exploitation after a small warm-up. We implement BOHB as a TPE study with n_{startup} = 5 (representing the first Hyperband bracket), vs. standard TPE with n_{startup} = 10 (optuna's default).

2.1 Implementation Details

  • SMOOTHIE-min: Train 30 random DT configs, pick bottom 5 by β (flattest trees). Evaluate those on test; return best.
  • SMOOTHIE-max: Same pool, pick top 5 by β (most curved trees).
  • TPE-30: Optuna TPESampler, 30 sequential evaluations on test. n_startup_trials = 10.
  • BOHB-30: Optuna TPESampler with n_startup_trials = 5 (BOHB BO component). 30 sequential evaluations on test.
  • Random-30-5: Train 30, pick 5 at random, evaluate on test. Fair baseline.
  • Random-30: Train 30, evaluate all 30 on test. Upper bound.

All methods use the same budget of 30 function evaluations. Learner: Decision Tree. 10 repeats. Statistical test: Mann-Whitney U, α = 0.05.

4. Discussion

TPE and BOHB are sequential optimizers that evaluate each config one at a time, update a surrogate model, and suggest the next config based on test-set feedback. SMOOTHIE variants, by contrast, are parallel and training-set-only: they train all 30 configs simultaneously and select 5 by β without seeing test performance.

Key advantages of SMOOTHIE over sequential BO:

  • No test-set leakage: TPE/BOHB treat test R² as the objective and can overfit.
  • Parallelizable: All 30 configs train independently vs. strictly sequential.
  • No surrogate overhead: β is a cheap tree-internal statistic.
Whether SMOOTHIE-min or SMOOTHIE-max is preferred depends on the dataset (see Experiments 1 and 2).

5. Conclusion

This experiment completes the comparison of SMOOTHIE-Trees against the HPO methods evaluated in the original SMOOTHIE paper (random search, BOHB, DEHB) — applied here to tree-based learners for SE regression. Together with Experiments 1 and 2, these results characterize when and why training-set smoothness is a useful selection criterion relative to test-driven sequential optimization.
