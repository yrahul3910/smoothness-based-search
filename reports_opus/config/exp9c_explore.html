Experiment 9c: Screen — κ-bounds and surrogate alternatives

Date: 2026-05-25 · 4 datasets × 20 repeats · budget N=30 · ↩ Exp 9b (κ-sensitivity sweep) · ↩ Exp 10 (wide replication) · ↑ index

1. Two follow-up directions screened

Exp 9b found the SMAS β-controller is architecturally capped — κ_eff ∈ (0, κ_base) can't reach the high-exploration regimes some datasets seemingly prefer. Exp 10 found INGS ≥ SMAS at scale. Two natural follow-ups:

  1. Lower / upper κ bounds. Replace κ_eff = κ_base · sigmoid(z(β)) with κ_eff = κ_lo + (κ_hi − κ_lo) · sigmoid(z(β)). If the controller was held back by the cap, a wider range should let it pick up oracle-tuned per-dataset headroom we saw in Exp 9b.
  2. Surrogate alternatives. Replace the linear-kernel RBF with simpler/different predictors (linear regression, k-NN, random forest, GP-Matern). RBF was chosen heuristically in Exp 9 — better alternatives could exist.
Screen design. 4 datasets covering distinct regimes (X264 — smoothness-friendly; SS-A — small/low-dim; SS-W — large pool / TPE wins; SQL — high-dim / TPE wins); 20 repeats; budget=50; report win@N=30. Reference numbers below are from Exp 10's 50-repeat run on the same datasets (tighter than re-running).

The architectural-cap hypothesis from Exp 9b — that the controller would help with more κ headroom — is refuted by direct test. The β signal driving sigmoid(z(β)) is too noisy to make sensible cross-step explore/exploit decisions; giving it more range just lets it overshoot. Direction abandoned.

Result: RBF/linear is the best surrogate. Best: rbf_linear (INGS) (78.2). Worst: gp_matern (69.7). RBF wins decisively on SQL (high-dim, where parametric methods underfit with ~50 observations). Linear regression is a near-tie on X264 / SS-W but loses on SS-A / SQL. Random Forest is competitive but consistently a hair worse. Most surprising: Gaussian Process is the worst — Matern + auto-tuned length-scale can't get good fits with ~50 observations in these dimensions. Direction abandoned.

5. Why RBF/linear is hard to beat

The "linear" kernel RBF (φ(r) = r) is not dimensional regression — it's inverse-distance-weighted interpolation through observed points. That degrades gracefully in high-dim (SQL 39d) where parametric methods try to fit a global structure that doesn't exist with 50 points. It's a robust default precisely because it doesn't model — it just smooths between known values.

6. What this leaves open

Not pursued further on this thread, but if the smoothness-aware optimization is ever picked back up these are the next things I'd try:

  • Better anti-Laplacian. The current one-sided form (2·u(h) − u(nearest_revealed)) is approximate. Try k-nearest revealed neighbors averaged, or restrict invocation to candidates where the surrogate is confident.
  • Portfolio with TPE. TPE wins on high-dim/large-pool; INGS on smooth/small. A trivial portfolio that runs both and picks-by-validation could plausibly beat both.
  • Adaptive surrogate choice. Fit RBF + linear + RF each step and pick by leave-one-out CV on the observed set.
