Smoothness-Guided Optimization — Experiment Index

Extending SMOOTHIE (Yedida & Menzies, 2025) to gradient-free settings on SE datasets. Each round targets a different task and dataset family; the metric differs accordingly.

Datasets used throughout: Process = data/optimize/process/ (10 SE regression datasets: COC1000, NASA93, POM3a–d, XOMO flight/ground/osp/osp2). Config = data/optimize/config/ (8 software-config lookup tables: SS-A, SS-B, SS-I, SS-O, Apache, X264, SQL, HSMGP).

Round 1 — Establishing β-smoothness for trees and the INGS surrogate prior sessions process R²

Full reports at ../reports/. Round-1 work introduced the tree-β formulation and the INGS interpolation surrogate that Rounds 2 & 3 build on.

Round 2 — Surrogate-HPO on process datasets, fair-comparison reframing process R²

Tune a decision tree's hyperparameters to predict each dataset's aggregate (√Σobj²) target; score by test R². Replaces the Round-1 TPE-stand-in BOHB with real hpbandster BOHB and removes test-set peeking from the baselines.

6 Fair vs peeking TPE/BOHB/Random — re-run baselines with val-R² objective (no test leakage) Peeking advantage concentrated on tiny test sets (BOHB +0.20, Random +0.19 on nasa93dem 19 rows); ≤0.018 on large XOMO/POM. TPE is most peeking-robust. 1510 process
7 SMAS — INGS acquisition + adaptive β-term (sign & weight from pilot Spearman(β,val-R²)) Reaches parity with real BOHB: 2W/5T/3L vs Fair-BOHB, 0W/5T/5L vs peeking-BOHB (matches BOHB without seeing the test set). Loses to TPE 1W/1T/8L; ≈ INGS. 1510 process
8 SMAS-Screen — SMOOTHIE-style two-stage budget: cheap low-fidelity screen (80 configs at 5% subsample) → full-train top 8 Only ~1.6× faster than TPE (not 3–10× as in original SMOOTHIE); 0W/1T/9L on quality vs Fair-TPE. The cheap-screen trick transfers only partially to trees. 1510 process

Bottom line: SMAS reaches parity with real BOHB using only training data, but TPE remains the quality leader; the cheap-screen variant trades a small speed gain for measurable quality loss.

Round 3 — Direct configuration optimization on config datasets config distance-to-heaven

Search each software system's configuration space directly: pool-based active optimization over a lookup table; reveal up to N configs' d2h, return the best found. Methods set shrinks vs Round 2 because evaluating d2h IS the legitimate objective (no test peeking) and there is no fidelity axis (no BOHB).

10 Same 5 methods, expanded to all 24 SS-A–X + Apache/X264/SQL + Health-ClosedIssues (12 files concatenated → ~120k row pool). No subsampling. Scrum1k/10k/100k held back for an overnight run. The Exp 9 result moderates with wider coverage. INGS/SMAS still beat the no-smoothness Greedy ablation cleanly. Mean win@N=30: INGS 85.1, SMAS 83.7, TPE 77.8, Random 78.6, Greedy 76.7. But per-dataset W/T/L is near-even (SMAS vs TPE 11W/7T/10L; INGS vs TPE 14W/5T/9L). Only INGS reaches paired-Wilcoxon significance vs TPE, at N=20 (p=0.029). The "SMAS beats TPE 5W/1T/2L" headline from Exp 9 was driven by a favorable 8-dataset subset. 5028 (24 SS + 3 config + 1 HPO)

Bottom line (updated by Exp 10): The smoothness signal is real — INGS/SMAS comfortably beat the no-smoothness Greedy ablation everywhere. The TPE-beating claim from Exp 9 weakens to "competitive with, not dominant over" on the wider 28-dataset set; INGS is the only method reaching significance vs TPE (at N=20). TPE tends to win on the largest pools and highest dimensions (SS-X, SS-W, SQL, Health), where the RBF surrogate over only 50 observations can't model the response surface well.
